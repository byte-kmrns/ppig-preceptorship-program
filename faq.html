Frequently asked questions

Answers for students, providers, and referrals.

This page keeps common program questions in one place. PPIG should confirm any official school requirements before launching the site publicly.

For students

Who is eligible to participate?

First-year medical students seeking private practice shadowing may submit interest. Matching depends on preceptor availability and fit.

Does this guarantee a placement?

No. PPIG can collect interest and help coordinate introductions, but placement depends on willing preceptors, scheduling, and student fit.

Can I complete all 16 hours through one preceptor?

Possibly. Hours may be completed through one or multiple experiences depending on availability and official program rules.

How do I verify my hours?

Students should use the official school verification or reflection process. This mockup does not replace course or school requirements.

For providers

Who can serve as a preceptor?

PPIG is seeking DFW-area MD, DO, PA, and NP clinicians in private practice or independent outpatient settings.

What will students do during shadowing?

Students are observers. They may watch clinical workflow, patient communication, and practice operations while following clinic expectations.

How much time do preceptors need to offer?

Capacity can be flexible. A preceptor may offer a half day, full day, repeated sessions, or another arrangement that fits clinic flow.

Do preceptors need training?

Any required training or onboarding should be confirmed by PPIG and the school before launch. This page should not be treated as official policy.

Referrals and logistics

Can I refer a clinician?

Yes. A warm introduction is ideal, but a name, specialty, practice location, and contact route can still help PPIG start outreach.

What information should referrals include?

Include the clinician or practice name, specialty, provider type, DFW location, email or phone, and whether you can make an introduction.

What if a clinician is interested but unsure?

They can submit the provider interest page or email PPIG with questions before committing to a student placement.

Still have a question?

Email PPIG or submit the relevant sign-up page.
