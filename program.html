Program information

Private practice shadowing with a clearer structure.

The program is designed to help first-year medical students complete required shadowing hours while learning how private practices operate in real outpatient settings.

At a glance

  • 16 required shadowing hours
  • DFW-area private practice focus
  • MD, DO, PA, and NP preceptors
  • Student-preceptor matching through PPIG
1

Student interest

Students submit their availability, transportation constraints, specialty interests, and goals for the experience. PPIG uses that information to identify possible preceptor fits.

2

Preceptor fit

PPIG collects interest from private practice clinicians and checks whether their schedule, specialty, and student capacity align with the student pool.

3

Introduction

When there is a reasonable fit, PPIG can make an introduction so the student and preceptor can confirm timing, site expectations, and any required paperwork.

4

Shadowing

Students attend as observers, follow clinic policies, maintain professionalism, and stay within the limits of the shadowing role.

Student expectations

Professionalism first.

  • Arrive on time and communicate early if scheduling changes.
  • Follow all site rules, dress expectations, and privacy expectations.
  • Ask thoughtful questions when appropriate without disrupting clinic flow.
  • Confirm official hour verification requirements through the school process.

Preceptor expectations

Flexible observational exposure.

  • Host students only when clinic flow and patient comfort allow it.
  • Explain private practice workflow, staffing, scheduling, and operations when practical.
  • Let PPIG know your preferred days, capacity, and contact route.
  • Share any site-specific requirements before the student arrives.

Choose the right path.

Students can request a placement. Providers can offer a preceptorship or refer a clinician.
