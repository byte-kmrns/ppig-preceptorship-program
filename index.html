For students

Use shadowing time to understand the business and human side of care.

This initiative is for first-year students who want a practical private practice experience while working toward their required 16 shadowing hours.

  • Observe outpatient clinical flow and patient communication.
  • Learn how practices handle scheduling, staffing, billing pressure, and operations.
  • Build early mentorship relationships with local clinicians.
  • Complete hours with professionalism, punctuality, and respect for clinic policies.

For preceptors

Host curious students without turning it into a heavy administrative lift.

PPIG is looking for private practice clinicians in the DFW area who are open to hosting first-year students for observational shadowing.

  • Eligible preceptors may include MDs, DOs, PAs, and NPs.
  • Scheduling can be based on your clinic capacity and comfort level.
  • Students are expected to follow clinic rules, dress standards, and privacy expectations.
  • Your practice can help students see how independent care is actually delivered.

FAQ

Questions the site should answer before sign-up.

Does this guarantee a placement?

No. The program can collect interest and help coordinate introductions, but matching depends on preceptor availability, student fit, and scheduling.

Who can serve as a preceptor?

The initiative is seeking private practice MDs, DOs, PAs, and NPs in the DFW area who are willing to host first-year students for observational shadowing.

What should students expect?

Students should expect observational clinical exposure, professional conduct expectations, and a chance to ask thoughtful questions about practice operations when appropriate.

Can someone refer a clinician?

Yes. A warm introduction is ideal, but even a clinician name, specialty, practice location, and contact route can help PPIG start outreach.
